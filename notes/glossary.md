# Glossary

## Common Web3 and NFT Terms

### A
**Airdrop**
Free distribution of NFTs or tokens to a list
of wallet addresses as a reward or promotion.

### B
**Base**
An Ethereum Layer 2 blockchain built by Coinbase
that offers fast and cheap transactions.

**Basescan**
The block explorer for Base Mainnet where you
can view all transactions and contracts.

**Burn**
The permanent destruction of an NFT by sending
it to a dead address making it unrecoverable.

### C
**Contract**
A self executing program deployed on the blockchain
that runs exactly as programmed without any downtime.

### E
**ERC721**
The Ethereum standard for non fungible tokens
where each token is unique and not interchangeable.

**ERC2981**
The Ethereum standard for NFT royalty information
that tells marketplaces how much royalty to pay.

### G
**Gas**
A fee paid to the network to execute transactions
on the blockchain measured in ETH.

### M
**Mint**
The process of creating a new NFT on the blockchain
by calling the mint function on the contract.

### N
**NFT**
Non Fungible Token. A unique digital asset stored
on the blockchain that cannot be duplicated.

### O
**OpenZeppelin**
A library of secure and audited smart contract
templates used to build NFT and token contracts.

### P
**Presale**
An early minting phase reserved for whitelisted
addresses before the public sale begins.

### R
**Royalty**
A percentage of secondary sales paid back to the
original creator every time the NFT is resold.

### S
**Staking**
Locking an NFT in a contract to earn rewards
over time without selling the NFT.

**Solidity**
The programming language used to write smart
contracts on Ethereum and Base.

### W
**Whitelist**
A list of wallet addresses that are approved to
mint during the presale phase at a discounted price.
